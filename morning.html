What qualifies as a workout?

A series of exercises that are planned, organised, and goal-oriented. These goals vary from person to person and range from losing weight to strength building, improving flexibility, or conditioning the cardiovascular system. Not just physiological, workouts have tremendous benefits for our mind and help keep it agile. Workouts are developed for individuals, keeping in mind their age, exercise experience, and fitness levels, and can be classified into beginner, intermediate, and advanced levels. Workouts generally comprise strength, stretching, balance, and aerobic exercises designed to achieve specific objectives.



What makes morning workouts so special?

The amazing all-around benefits that we gain from workouts make it even more important to start the day with an early morning home workout. According to ancient yogic science, the pre-dawn time or the ‘Brahma Muhurta’ i.e between 3:30 am to 5:30 am is the time of the creator. It is believed the benefit of studying, praying, and exercising increase during this period. Scientifically also, for your health to be good morning workouts are recommended. When you workout in the morning, you do so on an empty stomach and bowels, which is imperative for exercising. Besides, your mind and body are less stressed and tired. This is the time when there are few distractions and you reap the benefits of exercise all day through improved metabolism and a healthier mindset.

How effectively can morning exercises be done at home?

The best advantage of a full body morning workout is that it can be completely customised according to space and equipment that you have at hand. Morning home workouts can be easily done without the need for special hi-tech equipment or a fancy studio or gym space. Here is how you can do your morning workout at home: Develop a plan for yourself keeping in mind your objectives and stick to it. To do your morning workouts properly, you can either learn them from an experienced trainer or good online channels. For your morning workouts, find a space in your house that’s well ventilated and lit. This is to ensure good energy and positivity. The space that you choose for your early morning workout must not be cluttered and should be clean and calm. For a good morning workout, switch off your phone and TV.

Who can do morning workouts?

A morning full body workout can be done by anyone who wants to be fit and healthy and increase stamina. These workouts can be specially customised and scaled up and down for: Seniors can easily do a gentle early morning workout. Children as young as 7 years old can do simple workouts in the morning. People who have experienced a recent injury and are healing can start with simple workouts in the morning to regain their strength. People who have undergone surgery and are recuperating can start simple morning workouts to regain physical fitness. Pregnant women can do simple and gentle morning workouts. Those who suffer from muscular weakness, fatigue, digestive issues, etc., can do morning yoga to gain strength.
